EDR

Endpoint Detection and Response

Description

Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR) refers to a set of security solutions designed to monitor endpoint devices for suspicious activities and provide real-time responses to threats. EDR tools collect data from endpoints, such as computers, servers, and mobile devices, to detect potential security incidents and anomalies. Unlike traditional antivirus software, which primarily focuses on known malware signatures, EDR solutions employ advanced analytics and machine learning to identify new, unknown threats based on behavior patterns and indicators of compromise (IOCs). The EDR systems not only detect threats but also enable security teams to respond quickly to incidents by isolating affected devices, investigating breaches, and remediating vulnerabilities. This capability is critical in today's landscape where cyber threats are continuously evolving. EDR is a key component of a comprehensive cybersecurity strategy, as it enhances visibility into endpoint activity, improves incident response times, and ultimately aids organizations in mitigating risk and protecting sensitive data.

Examples

  • CrowdStrike Falcon
  • Carbon Black EDR

Additional Information

  • EDR solutions often integrate with Security Information and Event Management (SIEM) systems.
  • They can provide forensic analysis capabilities to understand the scope and impact of security incidents.
