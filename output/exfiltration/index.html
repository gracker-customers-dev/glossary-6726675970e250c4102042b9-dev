Exfiltration

The unauthorized transfer of data from a system.

Description

Exfiltration refers to the process by which data is transferred from a system or network without authorization. This can occur through various means, including but not limited to, physical theft, network breaches, insider threats, or malicious software. In the context of emerging technologies and threat intelligence, exfiltration poses significant risks as it can lead to the loss of sensitive information, intellectual property, and personal data. Cybercriminals often use sophisticated techniques to bypass security measures, such as encryption, data masking, and network segmentation. The rise of cloud services and remote work has further expanded the attack surface, making detection and prevention of exfiltration more challenging. Organizations are increasingly investing in threat intelligence solutions that leverage machine learning and AI to identify unusual data transfer patterns and potential breaches in real time. Effective monitoring and response strategies are essential in mitigating the risks associated with data exfiltration, as well as maintaining compliance with data protection regulations.

Examples

  • A cyber attacker uses malware to steal customer data from a corporate database.
  • An employee copies sensitive files to a USB drive and takes it outside the organization.

Additional Information

  • Data exfiltration can occur over various channels, including email, FTP, or cloud storage services.
  • Implementing Data Loss Prevention (DLP) tools can help detect and prevent unauthorized data transfers.
