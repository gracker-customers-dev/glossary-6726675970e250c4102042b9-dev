MITRE ATT&CK

A knowledge base of adversary tactics and techniques based on real-world observations.

Description

MITRE ATT&CK (Adversarial Tactics, Techniques, and Common Knowledge) is an extensive framework that catalogs various tactics and techniques employed by cyber adversaries. It serves as a comprehensive knowledge base for understanding the behavior of threat actors and their methods of operation. ATT&CK is organized into multiple matrices that represent different platforms, including Windows, Linux, macOS, and mobile environments, making it a versatile tool for cybersecurity professionals. In the context of emerging technologies in threat intelligence, MITRE ATT&CK provides a structured approach to analyzing threats, allowing organizations to better prepare for, detect, and respond to cyber incidents. By mapping observed attacks to the ATT&CK framework, security teams can identify gaps in their defenses and prioritize mitigation strategies based on real-world threats. The framework is continuously updated with new techniques and tactics, reflecting the evolving nature of cyber threats and the need for organizations to adapt their security postures accordingly.

Examples

  • Use of phishing emails to gain initial access (T1566)
  • Credential dumping techniques to obtain user passwords (T1003)

Additional Information

  • Widely used by security teams to enhance threat detection and incident response.
  • Supported by a community of researchers and practitioners who contribute to its evolving content.
