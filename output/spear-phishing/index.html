Spear-Phishing

A targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific individual or organization.

Description

Spear-phishing is a sophisticated form of phishing that targets specific individuals or organizations, rather than casting a wide net like traditional phishing attacks. In this context, cybercriminals conduct thorough research on their targets, including their roles, relationships, and personal interests. This allows them to craft highly personalized messages that appear legitimate and trustworthy, often mimicking trusted sources such as colleagues or known contacts. The goal of spear-phishing is typically to deceive the target into divulging sensitive information, such as login credentials or financial data, or to install malware on the victim's device. With the rise of social media and other online platforms, attackers have more access to personal information, making spear-phishing increasingly prevalent and dangerous. Organizations must implement advanced threat intelligence measures and user education to combat this growing threat effectively.

Examples

  • An email that appears to be from a CEO requesting sensitive financial information from an employee.
  • A message that looks like it comes from a vendor, asking for a password reset to access an account.

Additional Information

  • Spear-phishing attacks often have higher success rates compared to generic phishing due to their tailored nature.
  • Organizations can use threat intelligence to identify and mitigate risks related to spear-phishing by monitoring for known attack patterns.
